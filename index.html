What's your process?

For every soundtrack I make, I always begin with an idea. Sometimes it's a melody, sometimes it's a chord progression, and sometimes it's nothing more than a feeling I want to elicit. Once I've got an idea in mind, I'll work on making a rapid prototype of the song, usually in the form of a repeating chorus. After that, all that's left to do is fine tuning and fleshing out the 'arc' of the song.       

  • "I have an idea!" 
    It always helps to start with a central concept that you'll structure your track around.   
  • Rapid Prototyping 
    The key word is "rapid". Cut corners, but cut them fast. Just don't accidentally cut yourself.  
  • Polishing the 'Fenders' 
    Yes, that is a pun. Since you speedran the prototyping phase, you should have lots of time to make adjustments.
